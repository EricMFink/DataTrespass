# State Data Trespass Laws & Federal Labor Preemption 

North Carolina and Arkansas have adopted nearly identical data trespass statutes that subject employees to civil liability for certain activity on the employer's premises, including unauthorized recording and data collection. These statutes are the latest iteration of state laws aimed at data trespass. They differ from earlier Ag Gag laws in two significant respects. First, while Ag Gag laws exclusively to agricultural facilities, the North Carolina and Arkansas statutes apply broadly to all private premises, regardless of the type of facility or operations conducted there. Second, while most Ag Gag laws apply to unauthorized recording or data collection by anyone on the premises, the North Carolina and Arkansas statutes specifically target activity by employees. 

By imposing liability on workers who document and report unfair and unsafe conditions, these data trespass laws chill the exercise and enforcement of established rights under federal laws such as the NLRA, FLSA, and OSHA. Federal pre-emption should apply to prevent application of state data trespass laws in ways that would undermine federal protections.
